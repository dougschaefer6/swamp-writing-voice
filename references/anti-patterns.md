# Anti-Patterns

Organize by failure mode. Each failure mode gets:
1. A name (e.g., "The Template Voice," "The Vague Capability Claim")
2. A one-sentence description of the failure
3. A **Wrong** example (blockquoted)
4. A **Right** example (blockquoted)
5. A brief explanation of why the wrong version fails and the right version works

The wrong/right pairs should come from your organization's actual writing or

side but the right side should reflect how your organization actually talks.

Common failure modes to consider documenting:

- Generic template voice (interchangeable with any company's output)
- Purely positive claims (no acknowledged complexity or tradeoff)
- Vague capability claims (assertion without mechanism)
- Buried deviations (in RFPs, not disclosing where your response differs)
- AI giveaway phrases (constructions that read as machine-generated)
- Oversimplification (dumbing down technical content for a technical audience)
- Marketing escalation (superlatives and hyperbole instead of specifics)

End with a kill list of specific phrases to remove on sight. These are the
words and constructions that instantly mark output as AI-generated regardless
of the surrounding content. Every organization will have its own list, but
common entries include:

- "Leverage" as a verb
- "Robust" as a standalone adjective
- "Seamlessly" without explanation
- "It wasn't just X, it was Y"
- "Perhaps more importantly"
- "In today's [landscape/environment/world]"
- Em dashes used for parenthetical asides
- Three consecutive sentences with identical opening structure
