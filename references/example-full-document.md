# Example: Full Document

<!--
Paste a complete document that represents your voice at its best. This should
be a real deliverable, not a fabricated example. The whole point is to give the
AI a pattern to match against, and fabricated examples tend to drift toward
generic voice because there's no real context anchoring them.

Good candidates:
- A technical requirements document for a client deployment
- A complete SOW or proposal that won a deal
- A case study that your team is proud of
- An internal business case that got funded

After the document, add a section called "What This Document Demonstrates"
with 3-5 annotations explaining specific voice mechanics the document
exemplifies. These annotations are what teach the AI why the document works,
not just what it looks like.

Example annotation format:

**Technical specificity without oversimplification.** The paragraph about
[specific section] names the actual technical work rather than just claiming
the capability exists. A technical reader can evaluate this claim. A
non-technical reader understands that it was non-trivial.
-->
